# World and Story Bible

## Working Title

`HorseShooter: Equine Hate Saga`

## Protagonist

Name: player-chosen during new-game setup.

Identity:

- Adult woman.
- Long dark brown hair.
- Practical traveling coat, ammunition belts, worn boots, and weapon harness.
- Expert shot, terrible diplomat, unusually good settlement organizer.

Hard characterization and naming rules:

- The protagonist talks about how much she hates horses.
- The protagonist swears often.
- The protagonist does not become fond of horses.
- The protagonist can learn compassion for people, followers, towns, and communities, but her position on horses remains hostile.
- The story must never assert a fixed canonical first name, surname, nickname, or title that overrides the player-entered name.
- UI, saves, dialogue, journal entries, and follower conversations should use the chosen name only when it reads naturally.

Dialogue examples:

- "Another damn horse track. Of course. The world is a stable with worse plumbing."
- "I swear, if that horse looks at me funny, I am redecorating the road with it."
- "Good news: I found the problem. Bad news: it has hooves and an attitude."
- "I have met storms with better manners than that bastard horse."

## Tone

Darkly comic open-world RPG. The world treats horse disasters with mythic seriousness; the protagonist treats them with furious profanity and practical violence.

The comedy should not make the systems shallow. Towns can be damaged, people can be afraid, followers can disagree, and quests can have consequences. The protagonist's voice keeps the game sharp rather than weightless.

## World Premise

The continent of Bridlewaste was once built around trade roads, cavalry houses, ranch cities, and mountain passes. Then horses began changing.

Some became feral. Some became organized. Some became huge, armored, cursed, volcanic, spectral, or mechanical. Old cavalry orders collapsed. Trade routes broke. Towns isolated themselves. A half-understood force called the Great Wither appears to be turning horse populations into regional powers.

The protagonist does not care about the metaphysics at first. Horses ruined her life, horses ruin every road she walks, and she is going to shoot the problem until the world improves.

## Major Regions

1. Greenbarrow Grasslands
   - Starter region.
   - Open sightlines, ruined farms, roadside ambushes, first settlement.
   - Horse types: trotters, stampede runners, armored farm nags.

2. Gallowpine Forest
   - Dense paths, fog, vertical roots, hidden villages.
   - Horse types: antlered forest horses, silent stalkers, bark-armored chargers.

3. Frostreel Snowfields
   - Snow drifts, frozen rivers, abandoned watch posts.
   - Horse types: ice mane horses, sled raiders, frost breath chargers.

4. Blackglass Mountains
   - Cliffs, mines, bridges, wind tunnels.
   - Horse types: cliff leapers, iron-shod rams, avalanche herds.

5. Saltwake Coast
   - Ocean, docks, sea caves, wrecks, fishing towns.
   - Horse types: kelp-maned brine horses, dockside cargo smashers, tide bosses.

6. Cinderjaw Volcanic Belt
   - Lava fields, ash storms, basalt roads, forge settlements.
   - Horse types: ember horses, magma chargers, smoke mares.

7. Pale Spur Badlands
   - Desert, salt flats, canyon ambushes, old cavalry graves.
   - Horse types: bone horses, dust sprinters, mirage herds.

8. The Withered Paddock
   - Late-game corrupted region.
   - Twisted stables, impossible roads, horse cults, final faction war.
   - Horse types: cursed royal steeds, fused herd beasts, nightmare boss horses.

## Factions

1. The Roadless Guild
   - Merchants and scouts trying to restore trade.
   - Provides caravan quests and settlement trade upgrades.

2. The Bitless
   - Ex-cavalry soldiers who rejected horse rule.
   - Can become defenders or rivals depending on player choices.

3. The Hay Saints
   - Horse cultists who think the Great Wither is divine.
   - Major antagonist faction.

4. The Foundry Compact
   - Mountain and volcano craftspeople building anti-horse weapons.
   - Unlocks advanced weapons and settlement workshops.

5. The Tidewardens
   - Coastal militia protecting ports from brine horses.
   - Unlocks ocean routes and naval trade.

6. The Free Settlers
   - Ordinary people who join or resist the player character's settlement project.
   - Their trust determines city growth and ending outcomes.

## Main Story Arc

Act 1: The Road Is Full of Hooves

- The protagonist survives a horse raid.
- Player founds a camp.
- First boss: The Toll Mare, a horse blocking trade roads.
- Reveal: horse behavior is coordinated by regional "bridle marks."

Act 2: Build Something They Cannot Trample

- The protagonist expands the settlement.
- Factions compete for influence.
- Player explores forest, coast, mountain, and snow regions.
- Bosses begin affecting world state unless defeated.

Act 3: The Great Wither

- The horse plague is linked to old cavalry magic, industrial abuse, and faction betrayal.
- Player chooses alliances and settlement identity.
- Large battles can happen at roads, gates, docks, and volcanic forges.

Act 4: No More Damn Horses

- The protagonist enters the Withered Paddock.
- Final city state matters: militia, walls, followers, trade, morale, and research affect the final campaign.
- Final boss: The Crowned Stallion, a multi-phase horse monarch born from every bad decision Bridlewaste ever made.

## Quest Types

- Main quests: advance story acts and region unlocks.
- Settlement quests: recruit citizens, claim resources, defend districts, choose laws.
- Follower quests: loyalty, personal history, combat upgrades.
- Faction quests: alliances, betrayals, trade agreements, raids.
- Boss hunts: track, weaken, and defeat named horse threats.
- Road events: ambushes, caravans, refugees, broken bridges, horse nests.
- Exploration quests: ruins, caves, ocean wrecks, mountain passes.

## Settlement Fantasy

The player can found `Spitehold`, a camp that can grow into:

1. Camp
2. Outpost
3. Hamlet
4. Village
5. Town
6. Fortified city

Growth is based on:

- population,
- food,
- water,
- defense,
- morale,
- trade,
- workshops,
- follower assignments,
- regional threat,
- quest outcomes.

The protagonist complains constantly but becomes competent at building a place people can survive in.
