# HorseShooter Product Context

Register: product

## Product Purpose

`HorseShooter: Equine Hate Saga` is a Godot 4.6 Android open-world action RPG. The current wave shooter is only a prototype base. The target product is a mobile-first RPG with continuous regions, settlement growth, followers, faction consequences, varied horse enemies, boss hunts, and a player-named adult woman protagonist with long dark brown hair who constantly swears about hating horses.

## Users

Primary players are Android users who want a humorous but mechanically rich RPG that can be played in short sessions without feeling like a shallow arena shooter. They need reliable touch controls, readable combat telegraphs, fast menus, clear quest direction, and enough world density to reward exploration.

## Experience Principles

- Start from play, not marketing. Every first screen should move the player toward naming the character, continuing a save, changing settings, or entering the world.
- Mobile readability beats decorative cleverness. Text, controls, telegraphs, loot, and map markers must remain clear on 6-inch phones.
- Horse hatred is the comedic identity, but systems carry the game. Quests, settlement economics, followers, equipment, factions, biomes, and bosses must all change play.
- The world should feel authored first and procedural second. Procedural drawing and generated assets are allowed only when curated into readable, validated game content.
- Android performance is a product feature. Visual upgrades must carry quality tiers, predictable memory use, and headless-loadable scenes.

## Anti-References

- Not a level-based roguelite.
- Not a static arena shooter.
- Not a generic retro pixel game.
- Not a menu-heavy RPG where the world is only quest text.
- Not a joke game that relies on one repeated profanity bit instead of progression, exploration, and consequence.

## Success Criteria

- The player can explore multiple continuous regions, discover landmarks, fast travel, and see biome-specific weather and threats.
- The protagonist identity is visible in art and writing without using a canonical name.
- Combat supports readable aim, dodge, weak points, status effects, weapon variety, armor, and world consequences.
- Settlement founding grows from camp to city with raids, jobs, resources, trade, followers, and story outcomes.
- Graphics reach a polished stylized-realistic 2D/2.5D standard with lighting, weather, silhouettes, curated generated references, and Android-safe quality modes.
